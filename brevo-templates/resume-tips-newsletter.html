Tip #1

Stop Sending the Same Resume to Every Job

This is the single biggest mistake job seekers make. When you send one generic resume to 50 jobs, you're competing against people who tailored theirs to each role. ATS systems rank resumes by keyword match — and a tailored resume can score 40-60% higher than a generic one.

What to do: Open the job description. Copy it. Paste it into our ATS Keyword Analyzer. Then update your resume with the missing keywords before you apply. 10 minutes of work can be the difference between silence and an interview.

Tip #2

Replace Duties with Achievements

"Responsible for managing a team" tells a recruiter nothing. "Led a team of 8 engineers that shipped a product 2 weeks ahead of schedule, saving $50K in contractor costs" tells them everything.

The formula: Action verb + what you did + measurable result. If you can't think of numbers, use our AI — it's trained to turn weak bullet points into achievement-driven statements.

Tip #3

The 6-Second Rule is Real

Recruiters spend an average of 6-7 seconds scanning a resume before deciding to read further or move on. That means your top third needs to hit hard: a clear professional title, your strongest 2-3 skills, and one achievement that makes them stop scrolling.

Quick test: Open your resume and cover everything below the first third. Would you interview that person based on what's visible? If not, let's fix it.
